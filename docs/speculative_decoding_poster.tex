{\LARGE MLSys Course Project}

\posterbox{
{\Huge\bfseries Main takeaway.}
{\Huge A middle verifier is helpful when it improves answer quality. On GSM8K, our three-layer Qwen stack consistently improves exact-match accuracy over the tuned two-layer speculative baseline.}
}

\begin{multicols}{3}

\blocktitle{Question}

Speculative decoding uses a small model to draft tokens and a large model to verify them. We ask whether inserting a middle verifier helps:

\vspace{0.1in}
\begin{center}
{\LARGE Small drafter $\rightarrow$ Middle verifier $\rightarrow$ Large verifier}
\end{center}

Our tested stack:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Tier & Model & Role \\
\midrule
Small & Qwen2.5-0.5B & draft \\
Middle & Qwen2.5-1.5B & filter/route \\
Large & Qwen2.5-14B & final verify \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\blocktitle{Method}

The fixed-window hierarchy lets the middle model filter small-model drafts before final verification:

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[node distance=1.65in, every node/.style={draw, rounded corners, align=center, minimum width=1.35in, minimum height=0.55in, font=\Large}]
\node (p) {Prompt\\prefix};
\node[right of=p] (s) {Small\\drafts};
\node[right of=s] (m) {Middle\\filters};
\node[right of=m] (l) {Large\\verifies};
\draw[->, line width=1.3pt] (p) -- (s);
\draw[->, line width=1.3pt] (s) -- (m);
\draw[->, line width=1.3pt] (m) -- (l);
\draw[->, line width=1.3pt] (l.south) .. controls +(0,-0.7) and +(0,-0.7) .. (p.south);
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

Variants:

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{baseline\_sw5}: tuned two-layer baseline.
\item \texttt{fixed\_cascade}: always use the middle verifier.
\item \texttt{adaptive}: adjust windows using recent utility.
\item \texttt{selective}: route through the middle only when useful.
\item \texttt{cost-aware selective}: final in-progress run that bypasses the middle when measured utility is negative.
\end{itemize}

\blocktitle{GSM8K Paired Result}

On the same 1319 GSM8K samples, the three-layer hierarchy improves exact match by 8.64 percentage points and slightly improves throughput.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
Method & Tok/s & Score \\
\midrule
Two-layer baseline & 29.677 & 0.3010 \\
Three-layer hierarchy & 30.052 & 0.3874 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\posterbox{
{\LARGE This is the strongest evidence that the middle verifier is helpful: it changes the candidate trajectory and improves reasoning accuracy.}
}

\blocktitle{Tuned Baseline Ablation}

Against the stronger tuned baseline, all completed three-layer methods still improve GSM8K exact match.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
Method & Tok/s & Score \\
\midrule
\texttt{baseline\_sw5} & 34.387 & 0.3541 \\
\texttt{fixed\_cascade} & 31.935 & 0.3827 \\
\texttt{adaptive} & 32.816 & 0.3654 \\
\texttt{selective} & 33.265 & 0.3706 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The selective method is the best completed speed-quality compromise: it keeps most baseline throughput and improves exact match by 1.65 percentage points.

\blocktitle{Why It Helps}

GSM8K requires multi-step reasoning. A small model can make early draft mistakes that push generation onto a worse trajectory. The middle verifier is useful because it can reject or redirect those weak continuations before final large-model verification.

\posterbox{
{\LARGE Design lesson: use the middle model as a selective quality filter, not as an always-on extra verifier.}
}

\blocktitle{Conclusion}

{\LARGE Three-tier speculative decoding is helpful when evaluated as a quality-improving filter. On GSM8K, the middle verifier consistently improves exact-match accuracy. Selective and cost-aware routing are the practical path to keeping that quality gain while controlling overhead.}
